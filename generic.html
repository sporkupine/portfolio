About Me

My name is Connor Thompson, and I am a JavaScript/TypeScript developer from Paducah, Kentucky. I am also the owner and manager of Paducah Coffee Company, where I patiently endeavor in my endless quest to make the perfect cup of coffee.

My journey as a software developer began in college, near the beginning of the Bitcoin craze. With a little help from my friends, I was convinced cryptocurrency and blockchain technology would be the “next big thing,” and I set out to learn and absorb everything I could. This led me very quickly to the basics of computer science. Before I realized I was hooked, I had bought a couple of Udemy courses, installed a virtual machine to run Linux on my school laptop, and dug up my old Myspace page to remember anything I had ever learned about HTML (it wasn’t much, but you have to start somewhere).

Fast forward a year or so into the process of teaching myself software development and computer science (primarily through the excellent resources at The Odin Project and FreeCodeCamp), and I found myself stuck in a rut. I could code along with tutorials, explain the basics of JavaScript, and build small front-end projects for aesthetic and time-wasting purposes (you can see a couple of them here! ), but I was without direction, rudderless in a vast and overwhelming ocean of information.

It was then that I stumbled across the CodeLabs full-stack developer training program, being newly offered in my home town of Paducah. CodeLabs is an adult education program provided by CodeFi and geared towards providing working adults in rural areas like western Kentucky with the tools they need to transition into software development jobs, and thereby attract more employers to the area. You can read more about them here. In the front-end portion of the CodeLabs program, I have begun the process of mastering the Angular TypeScript framework. Learning both a new framework and a new language simultaneously was challenging but rewarding, and I now have all the tools of Angular 13 at my disposal to build attractive, responsive, and useful web applications.

The next step in my journey will be the back-end portion of the CodeLabs program, in which I will learn the fundamentals of server-side programming and master Ruby on Rails. By April 2022, I will be a professional-level full-stack developer looking to further my knowledge with new technologies and frameworks while seeking opportunities to educate new programmers and develop the budding technology industry of western Kentucky.
